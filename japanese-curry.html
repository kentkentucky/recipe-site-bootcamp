Japanese Curry Rice

Japanese Curry Rice (カレーライス, Kare Raisu) is a beloved comfort dish in Japan, characterized by its rich, savory curry sauce served over steamed rice. Unlike the spicier curries found in India or Thailand, Japanese curry has a milder, slightly sweet flavor, often thickened with a roux made from flour and butter. The dish typically includes ingredients like tender pieces of meat (usually chicken, beef, or pork), potatoes, carrots, and onions, making it hearty and satisfying.

Japanese curry rice became popular in the late 19th century after being introduced by the British, who had adopted curry during their time in India. Over time, it evolved to suit Japanese palates, with various regional twists and adaptations. Today, it’s a staple in homes and restaurants across the country, often served with pickled vegetables (fukujinzuke) on the side for a tangy contrast. Easy to prepare and endlessly customizable, Japanese curry rice is a true representation of Japan’s ability to transform foreign cuisine into something uniquely its own.

Instructions

  1. Start to cook rice.
  2. Cut the meat into bite size pieces and set aside.
  3. Peel and cut the potatoes, carrot, and onion into large bite size pieces and set aside.
  4. Peel and slice the garlic.
  5. Heat olive oil in a pot over medium heat and add garlic
  6. When the garlic is fragrant, add the onion to cook.
  7. Add the protein and cook till browned, then add potato, carrot, and onion and stir with a wooden spatula.
  8. When the oil has coated evenly on all the ingredients and the outer-edge of the potatoes have become transparent, add the water, honey, and grated apple.
  9. Bring it to boil and then turn the heat down to low to simmer until the potatoes become soft. and all the other ingredients are cooked. (Takes about 15 minutes)
  10. Turn the heat off, break the curry sauce blocks apart and add to the pot.
  11. Make each piece of curry roux dissolve into the cooking water in ladle with a pair of chopsticks
  12. Turn the heat back on low and bring to simmer to thicken the sauce.
  13. Serve cooked plain rice in a shallow bowl and pour the curry over the rice.
  14. Garnish with Fukushinduke and Rakkyo if you like. (Optional)
